I am a junior undergraduate student at the School of Microelectronics, South China University of Technology (2023.09-2027.06). I am now spending the Spring 2026 semester as an exchange student at the University of California, Berkeley, where I am taking courses in computer architecture and digital integrated circuits (2026.01-2026.05).

I built my foundational expertise in transistor-level RFIC design, focusing on physical layout and device-level performance. However, as I pushed for higher performance, I increasingly encountered the developmental bottlenecks of traditional RFIC design. I realized that its heavy reliance on rigid, incremental circuit-level optimizations offers limited scalability and is struggling to keep pace with the rapidly evolving, model-driven computing landscape. This sense of structural limitation was fully crystallized during my participation in the [EDA Forum 2025](https://events.hkust-gz.edu.cn/en_GB/detail/4436) and the [Advanced Chip Forum 2025](https://chip-forum.com/chip2025.html) at HKUST(GZ). Engaging in deep discussions about the structural dissonance between conventional sequential processing paradigms and spatially-distributed, tensor-based AI dataflows fundamentally shifted my perspective. Coupled with hands-on explorations of the severe computational bottlenecks these models face on conventional hardware, it became clear that the future of computing demands architectural innovation rather than just device-level tweaking. This naturally drew my focus toward computer architecture, a field where system-level design offers a much more powerful and scalable lever for breakthroughs.

Today, my research vision is rooted in hardware-software co-design and Domain-Specific Architectures for next-generation generative and embodied AI. I am passionate about bridging the gap between high-level algorithmic ambition and physical silicon constraints. Ultimately, I strive to pioneer scalable system architectures that redefine the physical limits of high-performance AI computing. 🚀
