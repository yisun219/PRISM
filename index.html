About

I am a junior undergraduate student at the School of Microelectronics, South China University of Technology (2023.09-2027.06). I am now spending the Spring 2026 semester as an exchange student at the University of California, Berkeley, where I am taking graduate-level courses in computer architecture and digital integrated circuits (2026.01-2026.05).

I built my foundational expertise in transistor-level RFIC design, focusing on physical layout and device-level performance.However, my research trajectory evolved as I actively engaged with the broader academic community through advanced chip forums and hands-on explorations of AI models. Experiencing the severe computational bottlenecks these models create on conventional hardware, coupled with deep forum discussions on the structural limitations of traditional execution paradigms, fundamentally shifted my perspective. I realized that the future of AI computing cannot rely solely on incremental circuit-level optimizations; it demands architectural innovation. This realization naturally drew my focus toward computer architecture, a field where system-level design offers a much more scalable lever for addressing these challenges.

Today, my research taste is deeply rooted in hardware-software co-design and Domain-Specific Architectures for next-generation generative and embodied AI. I am passionate about bridging the gap between high-level algorithmic ambition and physical silicon constraints. Ultimately, I strive to pioneer scalable system architectures that redefine the physical limits of high-performance AI computing.🚀

Currently, I am doing meaningful projects with Prof. Jieru Zhao at School of Computer Science, Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU). During freshman and sophomore years, I had research experience with Prof. Xiang Yi, Prof. Li Gao, Prof. Guangyin Feng and Prof. Enyi Yao.

My current research focuses on computer architecture and computer systems, high performance AI chip, hardware-software co-design.😊
