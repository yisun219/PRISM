## Research Experience

**AutoSAG: Automating Spatial Accelerator Generation for Large AI Workloads**
*Advisor: Prof. Jieru Zhao, Shanghai Jiao Tong University | 09/2025 - Present*
- Propose an automated framework for generating spatial accelerators tailored to large-scale AI workloads, optimizing performance and resource utilization.
- Develop optimized mapping strategy and dataflow for Multi-Operator, resulting in improved data reuse and high hardware utilization.
- Reproduce the Gemmini framework to explore hardware-software co-design for accelerating matrix multiplication and AI workloads.

**TCN-Enhanced Transformer for Wideband Digital Predistortion of Power Amplifiers**
*Advisor: Prof. Xiang Yi & Prof. Enyi Yao, South China University of Technology | 03/2025 - 09/2025*
- Proposed a novel neural network based on temporal convolutional network and transformer for digital predistortion, representing the first framework applicable to both digital power amplifiers (DPA) and analog power amplifiers (APA).
- Benchmarked via the OpenDPD framework, demonstrating a 6.58 dB leap in ACPRc and a 2.14 dB gain in EVM compared to the best-performing DGRU model, effectively pushing the boundaries of PA linearization.

**Millimeter Wave Reconfigurable Low Noise Amplifier Design on 65nm CMOS Process**
*Advisor: Prof. Li Gao, South China University of Technology | 11/2024 - 10/2025*
- Developed a 28/39 GHz reconfigurable mm-Wave LNA in TSMC 65nm CMOS that delivers a 20 dB peak gain and a sub-3dB noise figure while maintaining S11 and S22 below -10 dB through optimized matching networks to balance high sensitivity with robust signal integrity.
- Explored magnetic-electric reconfigurable inter-stage matching network and output matching network with low Q loss when making reconfigurable functions.

**Analog Front End Design for High-speed Serial Interface Receiver**
*Advisor: Prof. Guangyin Feng, South China University of Technology | 03/2025 - 08/2025*
- Developed a 100Gb/s PAM-4 Analog Front End (AFE) in TSMC 65nm technology, integrating a first-stage CTLE and a two-stage VGA, demonstrating 3-12dB peaking at the Nyquist frequency through post-layout simulation.
- Extended system bandwidth by utilizing a transformer load in the first-stage VGA to introduce a new pole, achieving a low power envelope of 11mW.

**Receiver Design Based on Kramers-Kronig Relation**
*Advisor: Prof. Guangyin Feng, South China University of Technology | 09/2024 - 11/2024*
- Built a system-level MATLAB model of a Kramers-Kronig receiver, reproducing the architecture and performance of a synthesizer-free coherent receiver architecture (Ref: ISSCC2024 12.2).
- Investigated mixed-signal phase reconstruction techniques and develop a compensation algorithm to mitigate phase signal distortion.
